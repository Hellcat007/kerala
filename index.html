ABOUT KERALA

Kerala, a state located on the southwestern coast of India, is renowned for its unique cultural heritage, lush landscapes, and progressive social indicators. Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala boasts a diverse topography that includes serene beaches, tranquil backwaters, verdant hill stations, and extensive spice and tea plantations. The Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, runs along its eastern border, contributing to its rich biodiversity. The state is historically significant as a major spice trade hub, attracting traders from around the world, including Arabs, Chinese, and Europeans, which has left a lasting impact on its culture and cuisine. Kerala's cultural landscape is marked by classical art forms such as Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, as well as vibrant festivals like Onam and Vishu, which reflect the region's rich traditions and communal harmony. Kerala stands out in India for its high literacy rate, advanced healthcare system, and impressive Human Development Index. The state's literacy rate is over 96%, the highest in the country, and it has one of the best healthcare infrastructures in India, contributing to a life expectancy well above the national average. The economy of Kerala is diverse, with significant contributions from agriculture, fisheries, tourism, and remittances from the large expatriate community in the Gulf countries. The backwaters, particularly those in Alappuzha and Kumarakom, are major tourist attractions, offering unique houseboat cruises. Additionally, the state's emphasis on ecotourism promotes sustainable travel practices. Kerala's society is known for its progressive attitudes, including a strong emphasis on gender equality and social justice. This is reflected in its social policies and widespread education initiatives.
